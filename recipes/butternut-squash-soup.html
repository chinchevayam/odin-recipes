Butternut Squash Soup

Spicy Autumn Squash Soup: A Creamy Comfort

Step out of the chilly weather and into the warmth of a cozy bowl of autumn squash soup. A touch of spicy chili adds a comforting heat, while the addition of crème fraîche gives it a velvety smoothness.

    Ingredients
  • 1 butternut squash, about 2lb, peeled and deseeded
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 onions, diced
  • 1 garlic clove, thinly sliced
  • 2 mild red chillies, deseeded and finely chopped
  • 30oz hot vegetable stock
  • 4 tbsp crème fraîche, plus more to serve
    Steps
  1. Heat the oven to 400F/360F fan.
  2. Cut the squash into large cubes, about 1½in across, then toss in a large roasting tin with 1 tbsp of the olive oil.
  3. Roast for 30 mins, turning once during cooking, until golden and soft.
  4. While the butternut squash cooks, melt the butter with the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil in a large saucepan, then add the onions, garlic clove and three-quarters of the chillies.
  5. Tip the butternut squash into the pan, add the stock and crème fraîche, then whizz with a stick blender until smooth. For a really silky soup, put the soup into a liquidiser and blitz it in batches.
  6. Return to the pan, gently reheat, then season to taste. Serve the soup in bowls with swirls of crème fraîche and a scattering of the remaining chopped chilli.
